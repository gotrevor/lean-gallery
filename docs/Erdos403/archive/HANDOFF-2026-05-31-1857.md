the FNS "doubling transducer" (the documented next-grind for the lost Lin carry estimate). Doing it
computation-first revealed the carry estimate is **unnecessary**:

- A number is a sum of distinct factorials (indices ≥1) ⟺ all factorial-base digits
  `factDigit i n = (n/i!)%(i+1)` are ≤ 1 (repo's `FactBase.factDigit_factSum_le_one`).
- **Verified 3 ways** (full big-int to m=6·10⁴, modexp to 4·10⁶, exhaustive over one period):
  every `m ≥ 8` has an FNS digit ≥ 2 at index ≤ 11, in **both** `2^m` and `2^m−1`.
- This is a **fixed-modulus** fact: digit `i≤11` depends only on `n mod 12!`, and `2^m mod 12!` is
  periodic with period **1620** (`12!=1024·467775`, `ord₄₆₇₇₇₅(2)=1620`). So it's a finite
  `native_decide` over the window `[10,1630)` (+ m=8,9).

real sequence caps at index 11. They also conflated "general tied-pair carry is unbounded" (true)
