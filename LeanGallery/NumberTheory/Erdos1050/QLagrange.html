    The q-Lagrange identity (Borwein Lemma 2, "Piece IIIb") #

    With the q-Lagrange weights μ_j = ∏_{l≠j}(1 − q^l/q^j)⁻¹ (j,l ∈ [1,n]) and 1 < q, for any k:

    ∑_{j=1}^n  μ_j · (q^j)^k   =   q^k · [n+k−1, n−1]_q .

    This is the harder half of Borwein's first-form = second-form identity for the q-Padé denominator pₙ; together with the Cauchy expansion (cprod_cauchy) it gives pFirst n = pVal n, discharging the qLag hypothesis of Eterm_eq_pVal and the first clause of residue_open.

    Provenance: auto-formalized by Harmonic's Aristotle (run e53ca6e8), then ported onto the repo's qBin (which is the same q-Pascal recurrence Aristotle used) and verified axiom-clean in our kernel (#print axioms qLagrange = [propext, Classical.choice, Quot.sound]).

    The proof recognizes the LHS as a divided-difference sum Dsum x s m = ∑_j (x j)^m/∏_{l≠j}(x j−x l) of the monomial t^m at the nodes x j = q^j, which satisfies the recurrence Dsum_rec, vanishes in low degree (Dsum_low), and has the closed form Dsum_main by a double induction using q-Pascal.
