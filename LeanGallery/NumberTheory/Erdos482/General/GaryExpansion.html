    Base-g block orbits recover the base-g floor expansion of their value #

    The base-g (g ≥ 2) analogue of BinaryExpansion. A base-g block orbit is a sequence orbit : ℕ → ℤ with orbit 0 = m and orbit (n+1) = g·orbit n + dₙ, each digit dₙ ∈ {0,…,g-1}. Its real value is W = m + ∑ₖ dₖ·g^{−(k+1)}. Provided the digit sequence is not eventually all g-1 (htail: every tail contains a digit ≤ g-2 — excluding g-adic boundary values), the orbit is exactly the base-g block orbit of W: ⌊W·gⁿ⌋ = orbit n for every n (gary_floor_eq).

    Bridge for the base-g self-referential capstones (BaseGFinish), turning a digit-reading recurrence orbit into a statement about the floor orbit ⌊W·gⁿ⌋ of its value.
