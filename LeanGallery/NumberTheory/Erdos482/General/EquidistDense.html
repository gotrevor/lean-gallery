    y ∈ U → f y ∈ S

    A continuous function confined on a dense orbit to a closed set is confined everywhere on an open set. If Set.range x is dense, f is continuous on an open U, S is closed, and f (x n) ∈ S whenever x n ∈ U, then f y ∈ S for all y ∈ U. (Relative density: U ∩ range x is dense in U, and f maps its relative closure into the closed S.) The clean contrapositive form of the cubic finish: a defect that is continuous and exceeds the digit window somewhere on an open box cannot be confined to that window along the dense orbit.
