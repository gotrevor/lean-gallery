    a.e.-W equidistribution of the general Tᵈ orbit 2ⁿ(W, αW, …, α^{d-1}W) #

    The degree-d analogue of CubicTorusEquidist (α = 2^{1/d}): for almost every real W, the orbit n ↦ (2ⁿ·αⁱ·W mod 1)_{i<d} ∈ Tᵈ is equidistributed, hence dense. The analytic engine (MultidimWeyl.weyl_criterion_torus, DELEngine.ae_comp_mul_left, DoublingEquidist.ae_doubling_weyl_tendsto_real) is already degree-agnostic; the only per-degree input is the ℤ-linear independence rpow_lin_indep_int (⇒ ξ = ∑ᵢ mᵢαⁱ ≠ 0 for m ≠ 0).
