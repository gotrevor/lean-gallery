    Base-g mantissa (Stoll [St05], §1) #

    For a real w > 0 and integer base g ≥ 2, the mantissa t = w / g^m with m = ⌊log_g w⌋ lies in [1, g). This is the normalisation feeding St05's recurrences (General/Thm13, Thm12).

    1 ≤ w / ↑g ^ ⌊Real.logb (↑g) w⌋ ∧ w / ↑g ^ ⌊Real.logb (↑g) w⌋ < ↑g

    The base-g mantissa lies in [1, g). m = ⌊log_g w⌋, t = w/g^m, then 1 ≤ t < g.

    theorem LeanGallery.NumberTheory.Erdos482.General.mantissa_reconstruct (g : ℕ) (hg : 2 ≤ g) (w : ℝ) :
    w / ↑g ^ ⌊Real.logb (↑g) w⌋ * ↑g ^ ⌊Real.logb (↑g) w⌋ = w

    Mantissa reconstruction. t · g^m = w (m = ⌊log_g w⌋, t = w/g^m): the mantissa scaled back by g^m recovers w. So w and t have the same base-g digits up to the shift by m.
