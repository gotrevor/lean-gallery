    Erdős #482 — Graham–Pollak: the recurrence extracts the binary digits of √2 #

    u 0 = 1, u (n+1) = ⌊√2 · (u n + 1/2)⌋. Then u (2n+1) − 2·u (2n−1) is the n-th binary digit of √2. Source: Stoll, A fancy way to obtain the binary digits of 759250125√2, arXiv:0902.4168 (free); orig. Graham–Pollak, Math. Mag. 43 (1970) 143–145.
